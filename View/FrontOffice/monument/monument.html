Tunisian Cultural Monuments

Carthage Ruins

Location: Carthage, Tunis

Once a powerful city of the ancient Carthaginian Empire, the ruins of Carthage offer insight into Tunisia's rich history and cultural legacy.

El Djem Amphitheater

Location: El Djem, Mahdia

One of the largest amphitheaters in the Roman world, El Djem is an impressive example of Roman architecture and engineering in Tunisia.

Ribat of Monastir

Location: Monastir

This fortified monastery served as both a religious site and a defensive structure, reflecting Tunisia's complex historical landscape.

Kairouan Mosque

Location: Kairouan

Also known as the Great Mosque of Kairouan, it is one of the most important mosques in North Africa and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
